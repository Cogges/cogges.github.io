From the CrossTalk ...

Healing touch...

"Father Jan Lavric from Clayton, near Doncaster, had helped to organise a pilgrimage to Rome for his Parish. The highlight of the trip was an audience with the Pope in the Vatican. By the time Father Lavric had seen to the needs of his party he was exhausted and there was only one seat left -- a wheel chair!

No sooner had he sat down than a nun wheeled him off to be blessed by the Pope. Father Lavric had no chance to explain, the Pope said words of blessing, then left the room. As Father Lavric got up from the wheel chair, to see to his party, there was a tremendous commotion among the nuns and the rest, as they say, is history!"

Do you believe that people get healed and if you do why is it that some do get healed and some don't?

If you've ever been fortunate enough to sit in St Mary's church at sunrise -- when it is possible to see the sun rise that is -- you can't fail to notice the incandescent light stream through the stain glass of the beautiful east window. It holds your gaze for minutes and it's only then that you begin to notice what the rich colour of the glass is illustrating. All three windows show Jesus healing people with a touch of his hand -- truly inspiring.

In the time of Jesus those with disease would have been classed as unclean and pushed to the margins of society, many having to leave their homes and live outside their town. To touch such as these would have left that person unclean also. But again and again Jesus acted out of compassion -- with little regard for his own welfare he touched those with leprosy and afflicted with blindness, not only bringing physical restoration but allowing them to re-integrate into society.

The Gospel writers capture the healing ministry of Jesus in four dimensions:

  • The Spiritual -- He claimed that a restored relationship with God was possible through Him.
  • The Psychological -- He claimed He had come to set the captives free -- to enable the personality to be fully integrated.
  • The Social -- meetings with Jesus often lead to restoration of relationships.
  • The Physical -- 25% of the Gospels record Jesus healing people physically.

Sadly, I am confronted with illness often in my job and have experienced the impact cancer has on a family when my wife Cathy was diagnosed with Hodgkin's Disease seven years ago. It's really hard -- hard to watch someone you love suffer, hard to pray, hard to understand when prayers don't lead to someone being healed. But Jesus urged his followers to pray for healing and this has been the church's tradition through the centuries.

The local church at its best ought to bring comfort to the broken-hearted and be committed in prayer for the sick -- at its best, whatever the capacity for human suffering, the church should have the capacity for healing and wholeness in all dimensions.


Andrew Sweeney
