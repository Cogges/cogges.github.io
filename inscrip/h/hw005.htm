Benefaction Board. Wood, framed and painted black with gold lettering.

NOURSE ' S CHARITY
IN AFFECTIONATE REMEMBRANCE OF THE
LATE REVEREND ARIS HENRY NOURSE
FOR MORE THAN FIVE YEARS INCUMBENT OF THE
PARISH OF COGGS IN THE COUNTY OF OXFORD
WHO DIED ON THE 12TH DAY OF FEBRUARY 1868
WHOSE GREATEST PLEASURE AND HAPPINESS
WHILST ON EARTH CONSISTED IN PERSONALLY
ENQUIRING INTO, AND RELIEVING THE WANTS
BOTH SPIRITUAL AND TEMPORAL OF HIS
POORER BRETHEREN
THE SUM OF TWO HUNDRED POUNDS
HAS BEEN INVESTED BY HIS WIDOW MRS
HENRIETTA, CHARLOTTE, NOURSE. IN
THE THREE PER CENT CONSOLS, IN THE NAMES
OF THE VICAR, AND CHURCHWARDENS, FOR THE
TIME BEING OF COGGES. THE INTEREST
ARISING THEREFROM TO BE DISTRIBUTED ON
EVERY TWELFTH DAY OF FEBRUARY, IN
TWELVE EQUAL SUMS .AMONGST TWELVE POOR
FAMILIES, RESIDENT PARISHIONERS OF
COGGES, HIGH COGGES, THE HEATH, AND
NEWLAND, WHO ARE REGULAR CHURCH PEOPLE
ATTENDING DIVINE SERVICE AT THE
PARISH CHURCH AT LEAST ONCE EVERY
SUNDAY UNLESS PREVENTED
BY ILLNESS.
THE FAMILIES TO BE SELECTED
BY THE VICAR AND HIS TWO CHURCHWARDENS
AND TO BE TAKEN IN SUCCESSION
NAMELY TWELVE FAMILIES ONE
YEAR AND ANOTHER TWELVE THE NEXT
YEAR, SO THAT ALL REALLY DESERVING
PARISHIONERS MAY BENEFIT IN TURN.
