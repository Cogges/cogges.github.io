A brass plaque commemorates the planting of an oak tree on glebe land due north of the lych gate in 1902.

THIS PLAQUE COMMEMORATES THE PLANTING
OF THE OAK TREE IN THE FIELD OPPOSITE
ALMOST DUE NORTH FROM THIS LYCH GATE.
IT WAS PLANTED TO MARK THE CORONATION
OF EDWARD VII IN 1902 BY J G PAYNE,
SECOND SON OF THE THEN VICAR OF COGGES,
REV. E. J. U. PAYNE.
